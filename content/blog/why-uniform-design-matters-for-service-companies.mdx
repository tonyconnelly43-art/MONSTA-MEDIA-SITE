By the time a technician knocks on the door, your van is already parked in the driveway and your website
has probably already been checked. The uniform is the last impression before a stranger lets your crew
into their house &mdash; and most service companies give it the least thought.

## It's a Trust Signal, Not Just a Shirt

A clean, branded uniform tells a homeowner this is a real, established company, not someone who showed up
in a plain t-shirt. That small signal matters more than most owners realize when it comes to first
impressions on the job site.

## Consistency Beats Decoration

The goal isn't to cram as much branding onto a shirt as possible. A logo placed consistently, in colors
that match your van and website, reads as more professional than a busy design with text in five
directions.

## Design for the Whole Crew, Not Just Techs

Office staff, sales, and techs all benefit from a consistent look, even if the specific pieces differ
&mdash; polos for the office, durable workwear for the field, matching hats and jackets for outdoor jobs.

## Build It Once, Reuse It Everywhere

A uniform system designed alongside your logo and van wrap means every new hire, every new season of
apparel, and every trade show booth uses the same assets instead of starting from scratch each time.
